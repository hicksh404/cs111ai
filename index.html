2024 Paris Olympics

Overview

The 2024 Paris Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad, will take place from July 26 to August 11, 2024. This prestigious event marks the return of the Summer Olympics to Paris after a century, having previously hosted the Games in 1924. With a vision to highlight the beauty of the French capital, the event will take place across iconic venues, showcasing Paris's rich culture and history.

Over 10,500 athletes from around the world will compete in 32 sports, including both traditional Olympic events and newer additions like skateboarding and surfing. The Paris Olympics aim to promote sustainability and inclusivity, making it a benchmark for future Games. The organizing committee is focused on reducing the carbon footprint and emphasizing local culture through the event.

Highlights and Events

The Olympics will feature a unique opening ceremony, taking place along the River Seine, an unprecedented setting that will allow spectators to view the parade of nations from the banks of the river. This innovative approach not only highlights the beauty of Paris but also aims to engage the public in a new way, making the Games accessible to millions.

In addition to traditional sports, the 2024 Games will emphasize sports that appeal to younger audiences, such as breaking, which will make its Olympic debut. With an increased focus on gender equality, the Paris Olympics will feature an equal number of male and female competitors, promoting a more inclusive environment for all athletes.
